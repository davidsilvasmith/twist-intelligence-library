E2273 - Anthropic’s Mythos is a cyber-weapon, so you can’t have it

Core Question

Where does defensibility move when frontier model capability becomes dangerous or commoditized?

Thesis

The strategic edge shifts from pure model spectacle to control of access, orchestration, routing, and inference-time execution. Dangerous capability changes distribution rules, while systems advantage becomes the operating moat.

Axioms

  • Capability risk turns go-to-market into access control.
  • The systems layer often compounds longer than the model layer.
  • In applied AI, inference-time efficiency can outrun training-time prestige.
  • Strategic containment is itself a market signal.

Decision Rules

  • If a capability meaningfully increases misuse risk, distribution must be treated as a security decision.
  • If a company depends only on model superiority, moat decay is likely.
  • If inference routing, specialization, and cost control improve output economics, that layer deserves strategic focus.

Proof Points

  • Mythos is framed through Project Glasswing, which moves the story from launch theater to strategic containment.
  • Rob May’s key lesson is that inference-time compute can become the stronger moat.
  • The battleground shifts toward routing, specialization, orchestration, and execution efficiency.

Contrarian Take

The most valuable AI companies may not be the ones with the loudest frontier model claims. They may be the ones that best control access and economics at the systems layer.

Operator Playbook

  • Map where your product depends on raw model capability versus orchestration quality.
  • Reduce inference waste before chasing broader capability.
  • Treat access policy as product strategy when risk is non-trivial.
  • Build differentiated execution paths, not just model wrappers.

Watchouts

  • Do not assume frontier branding creates durable defensibility.
  • Do not ignore distribution risk when capability risk rises.
  • Do not over-invest in training narratives if routing economics are the real edge.

One-Line Formula

When models get dangerous or commoditized, control of access and inference economics becomes the moat.
