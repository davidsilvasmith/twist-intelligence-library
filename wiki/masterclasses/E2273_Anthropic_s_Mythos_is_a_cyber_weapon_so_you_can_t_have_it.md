# E2273 - Anthropic’s Mythos is a cyber-weapon, so you can’t have it

## 5-minute masterclass
This episode appears to frame Anthropic's Mythos not as a normal product release, but as infrastructure with offensive implications. The core angle is strategic asymmetry: once a model meaningfully amplifies cyber capability, access control stops being a standard growth question and becomes a governance question. That is the lens that makes the conversation valuable.

The second pillar is Rob May's Neurometric thesis. Instead of competing on raw model size, the smarter game is inference orchestration, model routing, and cost compression. If frontier labs keep tightening access, the winners downstream may be the teams that make model usage cheaper, narrower, and operationally sane for enterprises.

## Focus notes
- Anthropic Mythos framed as a cybersecurity threat surface
- Access control and model restriction as strategy, not PR
- Rob May / Neurometric focused on inference efficiency and enterprise practicality

## The Formula
- Treat frontier model access as critical infrastructure once misuse cost exceeds distribution upside
- The enterprise AI winner may be the one that reduces inference cost, not the one with the loudest model
